Why hard-constrain a neural network to a beam equation?

For the SciML + GenAI challenge, the goal was synthetic vibration data for drone-wing damage that a classifier could actually trust. An unconstrained generator can produce signals that look plausible but violate basic physics — energy that appears from nowhere, momentum that isn't conserved. Embedding the Euler–Bernoulli beam equation directly into the training loss turns that from a hope into a guarantee: every generated time-history satisfies the constraint by construction, not by chance. That distinction mattered more than any architecture choice — it's what let a 100,000+-signal synthetic corpus stand in for data from crashes that, thankfully, mostly haven't happened yet.
